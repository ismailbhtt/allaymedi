# Allay Medical Solutions — Photography Brief

## Brand feel, in one line

**Warm-clinical.** Expert hands. Real homes. Neighbors who care. Think of the polish of a Ro / Hims marketing shot — applied to a Lake Oswego family-run store serving the 55+ audience. Avoid stock clinical coldness.

## Deliverable summary

Two days of shooting. One day at the store, one day on-location (real homes, with permission), split across three photographers if needed:

- **Product (60-80 SKUs):** three angles per SKU on a neutral off-white sweep
- **Lifestyle (20-25 scenes):** real people using real equipment in real homes and in-store
- **Store interior (12-15 shots):** storefront, aisles, counter, fitting area, staff
- **Staff portraits (6-8):** founder + floor staff, at-work candid + one editorial portrait each

## Top-level shot list

### Product — 60-80 SKUs × 3 angles

Neutral off-white (#FAF6F1 swatch) sweep. Soft directional light (45° key, small fill, large silk). Slight shadow to imply form — don't kill the shadow, don't let it compete.

**Priority SKUs for photography day 1:**

- ResMed AirSense 11, AirMini (with mask displayed)
- ResMed AirFit P10 and F30i masks (full, and cushion detail)
- Nova Vibe 6 Rollator (full, folded, seat detail)
- Drive Transport Wheelchair (full, folded)
- Moen DN7110 shower chair
- Carex raised toilet seat with arms
- JOBST UltraSheer (pair shot + packaging)
- Medline Protection Plus briefs (discreet package shot, no model)
- Threshold ramp
- Grab bars (one bolt-mount, one suction-mount for contrast)

### Lifestyle — 20-25 scenes

Every lifestyle shot must show a real person. Cast from our customer base (we'll pay for permission + time). Outfits should read like regular Portland-metro residents, not stock models. Locations should be real homes (older Portland craftsman, a Lake Oswego mid-century, a West Linn ranch).

**Must-have scenes:**

- New CPAP user being fitted by our staff across the counter
- Caregiver helping parent out of a shower bench
- Adult using a rollator in a real Portland-area neighborhood sidewalk
- Knee scooter being used in a kitchen (post-op recovery)
- Hospital bed at home, actually set up in a bedroom (with permission)
- Family receiving a rental ramp delivery
- Ice cleats on boots, Oregon winter (shot in season)
- FSA/HSA card being swiped at our counter
- Measuring compression stockings on a seated customer
- Hands attaching a grab bar to a shower wall (stud finder visible)

### Store interior — 12-15 shots

- Exterior: storefront from the sidewalk, natural light, no car in frame
- Entrance door: clean shot with hours sign visible, wheelchair-access emphasized
- Aisles: wide shots of the mobility aisle, CPAP wall, bath safety display
- Counter: customer-eye and staff-eye angles, include FSA/HSA signage
- Rental equipment area: hospital bed demo, wheelchairs queued for pickup
- Notary and sharps disposal stations
- Staff fitting a mask at the dedicated CPAP fitting table

### Staff portraits — 6-8 people

- One editorial: eye-contact, neutral background, soft key light, on-brand outfit
- One in-the-act: helping a customer, fitting a mask, adjusting a walker
- Small group shot for the "About" page

## Technical specs

- **Aspect ratios to capture:** 4:3 (primary), 16:9 (hero), 1:1 (social), 9:16 (vertical lifestyle for reels)
- **Resolution:** 6000px+ on the long edge, delivered as raw + high-JPEG
- **Color:** sRGB export. Neutral white balance — do not warm-shift excessively. Skin tones natural.
- **Consistency:** Same hour-of-day for exteriors. Same key light pattern across lifestyle set.

## What to avoid

- Stock-model handshakes, fake smiles, green-screen composites
- Overly warm orange tones (often added in medical retail photography — reads dated)
- Symbols of sickness (hospital gowns, IV bags, stethoscopes around necks of models who aren't nurses)
- Ageist or paternalistic framing (no "poor grandma being helped")
- Brand logos other than the ones we partner with (ResMed, Medline, Nova, Pride, JOBST)
- Medical waste, used supplies, anything that could read unsanitary

## Permissions & consent

Every customer or staff member in a lifestyle or portrait photo signs a model release. HIPAA doesn't apply to retail — but dignity does. If someone's equipment is visible and identifiable as theirs, get explicit consent even if the face isn't shown.

## Delivery

- Raw + edited JPEG to Dropbox (folder structure: `/allay/YYYY-MM-DD/[category]/`)
- Edited selects tagged with keywords matching the Sanity schema: product name, category, service, location, etc.
- Rights: perpetual, unlimited use for Allay web, print, and paid advertising. Model releases on file.
- Deliver within 10 business days of each shoot day.
